constitution

    CHUKA UNIVERSITY NUTRITION AND DIETETICS ASSOCIATION (CUNDSA)




    CHAPTER ONE: THE ASSOCIATION



    1.1 a) Name: the name of the Association shall be called "CHUKA UNIVERSITY NUTRITION AND DIETETICS ASSOCIATION ".

    b) Interpretation: i) “Association" means Chuka University Nutrition and Dietetics Association. ii) "Committee" means Executive Committee. iii) "Member" means a person registered as a member of Chuka University Nutrition and Dietetics Association.

    1.2 Vision

    To be an authority and resource for promoting good nutrition.

    1.3 Mission

    Empowering members to be leaders in the practice of nutrition and dietetics in promotion of the nutritional well-being and health of the population.

    1.4 Objectives

    The objectives of the Association are: - a) To bring the nutrition and dietetics students together. b) To promote education of individuals on health concerns common to the university fraternity and their prevention, through activities such as medical camps and other health awareness events. c) To promote social relations, social integration and understanding of social diversity. d) To organize health forums and other Association programs as regulated by the Association’s constitution. e) To promote the welfare of members



    CHAPTER TWO: ASSOCIATION REGISTRATION



    2.1 Association Registration and Approval


    a) The registration of the Association shall be sought by writing an official letter requesting registration to the Dean of students through the Patron, the COD (Chair of Department) and the Dean of the faculty.
    b) The association shall be considered registered and active upon getting approval by the Dean of students.



    CHAPTER THREE: MEMBERSHIP



    3.1 Membership Eligibility


    A person shall become a member of CUNDSA upon meeting the following criteria.
    a) Be a student pursuing human nutrition and dietetics course at Chuka University (Certificate, Diploma and Degree Level).
    b) Upon meeting (a) above, membership shall be considered valid upon payment of a registration fee of Ksh 500 and which shall be paid once during registration.
    c) Every member shall pay a semester subscription fee of Kshs, 100/= not later than the 6th week of each semester.

    3.2 Rights and Responsibilities of Members

    A member shall have the right and responsibility to: a) Participate in all the association activities b) Share information and ideas to the Association. c) Elect and be elected democratically to the committee of the Association subject to the provision of the Association constitution. d) Vote on all matters put before a meeting of the Association e) Respect and uphold the constitution.

    3.3 Cessation of Membership

    A person shall lose membership to CUNDSA upon:
    a) Failure to remain in good standing with the university.
    i) If one is suspended from university.
    ii) If one is discontinued with studies by the university.
    b) Failure to abide by the Association’s purpose and rules as stated in this constitution.
    c) Failure to subscribe for a semester while in session by the 6 weeks of the semester unless with a valid reason acceptable by at least two thirds of the executive committee members.
    d) Any member desiring to resign from the Association shall submit his/her resignation to the Secretary. i) The resignation shall take effect from the date of the committee's approval. ii) Any member who resigns from membership shall not be entitled to refund of his/her subscription or any part thereof or any monies contributed by him/her at any time. iii) Any member who willfully withdraws from the Association shall be eligible to re-joining the Association subject to payment of any outstanding balance the member had at the time of resignation. The member shall also be treated as a new registrant and will be expected to pay a new registration fee as prescribed by the constitution.
    e) Completion of studies; upon completion of studies, a member shall cease being a member of the Association. However, the member will automatically become a member of the CUNDSA alumni group. Members of the CUNDSA alumni group may participate in the Association activities but they will not participate in the Association voting or decision making.

    3.4 Disciplinary Action

    The Association executive committee will handle all the displinary actions concerning members of the committee in the presence of the patron.



    CHAPTER FOUR: LEADERSHIP AND MANAGEMENT

    CUNDSA shall be governed by a management committee

    4.1 CUNDSA Executive Committee Members

    The CUNDSA executive shall comprise of the following:
    i) The patron
    ii) Chair person
    iii)Vice chair
    iv) Secretary
    v) Secretary general
    vi) Organizing secretary vii) Treasurer viii) Auditor general ix) Welfare director x) Class representative to the Association from each class.

    4.2 Duties of the Executive Committee



    a) Coordinate and control the Association. b) Organize Association activities for each semester. c) Monitor and communicate to members the Association’s progress. d) Serve the Association with utmost good faith. e) Perform any other duties as deemed necessary to the best interest of the Association f) Appoint and release the patron. g) Keep the patron updated on the progress of the Association. h) Handle disciplinary issue in the Association and involve the patron if need be.

    4.3 Duties of Specific Executive Members



    4.3.1 The Patron
    The Head of Nutrition and Dietetics Section (HNDS) shall, by virtue of his/her official position be the Patron of the Association. The duties of the patron are; i) Oversee the running of the Association. ii) Link the Association with the university management. iii)Provide moral and professional guidance to the executive. iv) Induct new office bearers. v) Be present on the disciplinary meetings. vi) Perform any other duty for the benefit of the Association.

    4.3.2 Chairperson
    i)The chairman shall, unless prevented by illness or other sufficient cause, preside over all meetings of the committee and all members meetings. ii) Maintain order in the Association. iii)Represent the Association in all official events and be the official spokesperson. iv) Inform the patron on the progress of the Association. v) Be one of the signatories in the Association’s financial account documents. vi) Perform any other function assigned by the Association. vii) Call off the meeting if need be.

    4.3.3 Vice Chairperson
    i) The Vice-Chairperson shall perform any duties of the chairperson in his/her absence. ii) Perform any other duties assigned to him/her by chairperson or the executive committee.

    4.3.4 Secretary General
    i) Be one of the signatories of financial transactions to the Association. ii) Issue notices of all the registered and subscribed members. iii) Make formal communication to relevant officers in the university and beyond. iv) Perform any other duty assigned by the executive committee.

    4.3.5 Secretary
    i) Record keeping and read all the minutes of all meeting. ii) Have a record of all the registered and subscribed members iii) Any other duty assigned by the executive committee.

    4.3.6 Treasurer
    i) Collect all fees due to the Association. ii) Signs against all financial records. iii) Be one of the signatories of the financial transactions to the Association. iv) Give full financial reports twice in a semester. v) Perform any other duty assigned by the executive committee.

    4.3.6 Organizing Secretary
    i) Organize venues for the meetings, events and materials to be used for in time. ii) Be the custodian of the Association’s property. iii) Keep records of the Association’s activities. iv) Inform members of planned events and meetings on time. v) Perform any other duty assigned by the executive committee.

    4.3.7 Auditor General
    i) Audit financial records and give reports to the executive and members. ii) Perform any other duty assigned by the Association.

    4.3.8 Class Representative
    i) Keep all members updated on the progress of the meeting and Association activities

    4.3.9 Welfare Director
    i) Identify needs of the Association members. ii) Ensure the well-being of the members.

    4.4 EX-OFFICIAL MEMBERS
    There shall be ex -officio members of the Association who shall be; All previous executive



    CHAPTER FIVE: ASSOCIATION RESOURCES



    5.1 Association Resources

    Comprises all properties owned by the association in form of asset and capital.

    5.2 Sources of Finance

    a) Registration and subscription fees. b) Donations c) Projects d) Any other rightful source

    5.3 Accounts

    CUNDSA shall have a bank account.



    CHAPTER SIX: ELECTIONS



    6.1 Election Guidelines

    The following shall be the election guidelines a) Election of Association leaders shall be done every 2nd week of the first semester of every academic year. b) On election day all position shall be declared by the presiding officer. c) All fully registered and subscribed active members of the Association are eligible for election to any post as provided by the constitution. d) In case of a tie between two candidates the chairperson shall be empowered to get a second vote. e) Only registered and subscribed members shall be entitled to vote. f) Should vacancies occur before the completion of a term in office they will be a by-election by the executive to get a replacement.

    6.2 Eligibility and Qualification

    a) Must be active members of the Association; registered and subscribed to date. b) Shall contest for all positions without gender restriction

    6.3 Position Eligibility according to Year of Study

    a) From the 3rd years: chairperson, secretary general, organizing secretary, auditor generally, director welfare and class representative b) From the 2nd years: chairperson and vice chair, auditor general, organizing secretary, secretary, treasurer, director welfare and class representative c) From 1st years: secretary, organizing secretary, auditor general and class representation

    6.4 Handing Over

    a) Official handing over of newly elected officials shall take place within two weeks after the elections b) Handing over shall include all power and materials. c) All handing over shall be attended by the following: i) The patron or his/her designate ii) All incoming officials iii) All outgoing officials iv) All other members of the Association



    CHAPTER SEVEN: MEETING OF THE ASSOCIATION



    7.1 Meeting Guidelines;

    a) The executive shall meet at least once in two months. i) The quorum for the meeting shall not be less than 50 percent of the committee members. b) The Association members shall meet at least once per semester. c) The association will have an AGM at least a week prior to the Association elections. i) Quorum for the Annual General Meetings shall not be less than 50 percent of the registered members of the Association. d) Resolutions shall be decided by simple voting by a show of hands or secret ballot. In case of equal votes, the Chairperson shall have a second vote.



    CHAPTER EIGHT: CONSTITUTION AMMENDMENTS



    8.1 The following shall guide the CUNDSA constitution amendments;

    a. Suggestion for amendment shall be presented to the executive. b. The executive shall present the same to the members during the Association’s meeting c. The amendment must be approved by at least at least a two-thirds majority of the members present at a General meeting of the Association. d. The amendment shall be presented to the Dean of students though the Patron, COD and the faculty Dean. e. The amendment shall be come into effect upon approval by the Dean of students.
